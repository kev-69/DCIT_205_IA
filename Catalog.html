Courses Structure

Department Catalog

Upon gaining admission into the institution, Bachelor of Science in Information Technology are automatically admitted into the department. However, student who want to puruse BSc in Computer Science are tested to know whether they are deem fit for the program.

First Semester

Bachelor Of Science in Information Technology

Students under this discipline take 18-21 credit hours each semester. In Level 100, the department requires students to take only 15CH and additional 3CH from the UGRCs making a total of 18. If in any case the student feels he has the capacity to take more, he is allowed to take only 3 additional credits

The course structure for BSc in IT students are fixed for both semesters. For L100 semester 1, they include;

  • DCIT101 (Introduction to Computer Science)
  • DCIT103 (Office Productivity Tools)
  • DCIT105 (Math for IT Professionals)
  • STAT111 (Introduction to Statistics I)
  • ECON101 (Introduction to Economics I)
  • and a UGRC(either UGRC110(Academic Writing I) or UGRC150(Critical Thinking and Pratical Reasoning))

Bachelor Of Science in Mathematical Sciences

Unlike BSc in IT, you'll not be considered a computer science student automatically. Your results in both the 1st and 2nd semester in L100 will tell whether you fit or not. There are various other programs under this main program including BSc in Statistics, BSc in Mathematics, BSc in Acturial Science and BSc in Computer Science.

Students who want to pursue BSc in Computer Science, minor-major, major-minor or single major will take the following courses in the first semester;

  • DCIT101 (Introduction to Computer Science)
  • DCIT103 (Office Productivity Tools)
  • MATH121 (Algebra and Trigonometry)
  • MATH123 (Vectors and Geometry)
  • STAT111 (Introduction to Statistics I)
  • and a UGRC(either UGRC110(Academic Writing I) or UGRC150(Critical Thinking and Pratical Reasoning))

Second Semester

After a sucessful first semester, Students who want to pursue BSc Cs are still tested with some fewer course but the situationr remains unchanged for BSc IT stydents since it is a single major program

Bachelor Of Science in Information Technology

Just like semester 1, IT students continue with 6 courses and 1 additional optional course which is UGBS (Principles of Management).Below are the course to be taken in the second semester for IT students.

In the second semester, IT students are required to take Calculus I to prepare them towards Level 200 and Programming

  • DCIT 102 (Computer Hardware Fundamentals and Circuits)
  • DCIT 104 (Programming Fundamentals)
  • MATH122 (Calculus I)
  • ECON102 (Introduction to Economics II)
  • STAT112 (Introduction to Statistics and Probability II)
  • and a UGRC and a UGRC(either UGRC110(Academic Writing I) or UGRC150(Critical Thinking and Pratical Reasoning))

Bachelor Of Science in Mathematical Sciences

In the second semester, the Computer Science pursuits continue to take extra two mathematic course from the Department of Mathematics. Since the most part of Computer Science is algorithmic.

Below are the required courses for the BSc in Computer Science pursuits.

  • DCIT 102 (Computer Hardware Fundamentals and Circuits)
  • DCIT 104 (Programming Fundamentals)
  • MATH122 (Calculus I)
  • MATH126 (Algebra and Geometry)
  • STAT112 (Introduction to Statistics and Probability II)
  • and a UGRC and a UGRC(either UGRC110(Academic Writing I) or UGRC150(Critical Thinking and Pratical Reasoning))

Criteria For Program Allocation

After the completion of Level 100, Computer Science are well informed that they will need to obtain a grade point of A or B+ from all the DCIT, STAT and MATH courses take from both semester in order to be given the opportunity to pursue computer science in the subsequent years and obtain a degree in Computer Science.

However there are times where general performance are absurb and the course advisor would into consieration to lower to the grade point in the various courses into to enable students venture into the field.

Also student who did not obtain the required grade point would be given the opportunity to pursue programs like Major-Minor in Statistics and Computer Science, Major-Minor in Computer Science and Statistic, Major-Minor in Mathematics and Computer Science. This doesnt exclude them from being part of the department. Such students are to take 12CH from their various majors and 6CH from the minors

There are also student from other schools under the college who are also considered as members of the department. The include students from Physics-Computer Science. They are student majoring Physics and minoring Computer Science.
